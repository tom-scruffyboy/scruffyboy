01

the brand

scruffyboy makes premium grooming and cleanup goods for adventure dogs — the ones that come home muddy, beach-soaked, and extremely pleased with themselves. founded in hong kong. co-starring moose, a real dog, who is not a mascot and will not be turned into a cartoon.

the brand is a hyped modern consumer brand that happens to be for dogs. the tension between the scrappy name and the premium execution is the joke — protect it from both directions. never cutesy pet-store; never sterile luxury.

tagline
"grooming goods for dirty dogs." — always lowercase, always with the period.
the hard rule
no paws. no bones. no dog silhouettes. no pet-industry clichés. no gradients. anywhere, ever.
reference points
liquid death's confidence, graza's playfulness. deadpan, not zany.

05

voice

deadpan-playful. the humor comes from treating dog mess with the seriousness of a skincare brand, then undercutting it with one dry line. never puns, never baby talk, never exclamation marks doing the work a joke should do.

confident
state things flatly. "it works." beats "we think you'll love it!" — the brand never asks for approval.
specific
name the actual mess: trail mud, beach salt, "whatever that was." specificity is the premium signal.
dry
one joke per surface, delivered straight. if a line needs an emoji to land, it doesn't land.

06

photography

real dog, real dirt, real places. shoot moose (and friends) like an outdoor-gear brand shoots athletes: natural light, mid-action, unposed. the mess is the hero — never clean it up in post.

action — frozen mid-chaos, shot fast and close
aftermath — the problem the product exists for
product-in-world — gear context, never studio tabletop
always
  • natural light, real locations (trail, beach, car boot, bathtub)
  • dirt visible and celebrated
  • dog-height camera angles
  • candid over posed — outtakes are on-brand
never
  • studio seamless backgrounds or props
  • costumes, bandanas with logos, birthday hats
  • humans as the subject (hands are fine)
  • heavy filters or color grading that fights the palette
07

packaging

packaging is the brand's indoor voice: calm wordmark only, generous cream, one orange accent band. the shelf does the shouting through color blocking, not through the logo.

front label anatomy
scruffyboy
dirt & salt rinse
Rinses out trail mud, beach salt, and whatever that was.
for dirty dogs250 ml
  1. calm wordmark, top left, black on cream — never kinetic on pack, never centered
  2. product name in fredoka 600, lowercase, the largest element on the label
  3. one dry line of helvetica under the name — the only joke on the front
  4. orange accent band at the base carrying "for dirty dogs" + volume — the only orange on the front
  5. back label: helvetica only, sentence case, calm wordmark repeated small; regulatory text never in fredoka
product line naming: lowercase noun phrases that name the mess or the fix — "dirt & salt rinse", "post-swim wipes", "the hydrant" (paw cleaner). never names with "paw", "pup", or "fur" puns.
